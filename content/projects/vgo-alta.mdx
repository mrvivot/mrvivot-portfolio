context2En: "It all started with a call from the sales team to potential venues. Once it came back, someone from the team loaded everything into the platform by hand. The whole process took around two weeks, and that manual data entry alone ate up a full day of work per venue. My role was to design a system that took that team out of the loop: so the venue could sign up on its own, at its own pace, without relying on back-and-forth emails, and so all the submitted information would be integrated into the platform automatically."

  - number: "02"
    titleEs: "Diseño y prototipado"
    titleEn: "Design and prototyping"
    image: "/images/projects/vgo-alta/vgo-alta-proceso-02.png"
    descEs: "Trabajé junto al Product Owner de V-Go para ajustar el diseño a las necesidades del negocio. Ahí tomé una decisión con trade-off explícito: dividir el formulario en pasos bien claros, lo que significaba aumentar la cantidad de pantallas a completar, pero reducía drásticamente el margen de error. Sumé autoguardado después de cada paso, para que el usuario pudiera abandonar y retomar sin perder lo ya cargado. Iteré con wireframes de baja y media fidelidad, ajusté el flujo con el feedback del Product Owner, y construí un prototipo funcional en alta fidelidad que simulaba el proceso real de alta."
    descEn: "I worked with V-Go's Product Owner to adjust the design to the business's needs. That's where I made an explicit trade-off decision: splitting the form into clear, distinct steps, which meant more screens to complete, but drastically reduced the margin for error. I added autosave after each step, so users could leave and pick up later without losing what they'd already entered. I iterated with low and mid-fidelity wireframes, adjusted the flow based on the Product Owner's feedback, and built a functional high-fidelity prototype that simulated the real onboarding process."
